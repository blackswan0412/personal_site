Throughline

I study systems that turn distributed uncertainty into useful signal. The thread running through my work is this: how do you build infrastructure that makes collective reasoning legible and improvable?


My current answer has three parts. Prediction markets as the mechanism — they force honest belief expression and aggregate it into prices. Agent-based simulation as the laboratory — synthetic populations of forecasters let you stress-test market designs, study calibration failure, and run experiments you can't run on humans. Blockchains as the settlement layer — credible commitment, transparent rules, and permissionless access remove the friction that keeps prediction markets small and centralized.


Earlier work on social products, mental health AI, and consumer behavior gave me the human side of the equation: how incentive structures warp behavior, how people engage with uncertainty, and why so many coordination tools fail the people they're supposed to serve. That context is why I care about getting the mechanism design right — not just building markets, but building markets that work.
